Understanding Covered Call Option Strategies

Posted on November 10, 2025

Covered calls are a practical and conservative strategy that allows investors to generate income while maintaining ownership of their stocks. Ideal in stable or slightly bullish markets, the strategy combines income generation with disciplined risk control.

Example: You own 100 shares of XZY at $100 each. The stock now trades at $110, and you sell a call option with a $115 strike for a $1 premium. If XZY rises above $115, you sell the shares at your target price, plus the premium. If not, the option expires worthless, and you keep both your shares and the income.
